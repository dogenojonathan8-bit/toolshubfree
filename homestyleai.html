📋 Before You Start Any Home Project

5 things that protect your money every time.

☐ Get 3 contractor quotes before committingLearn ▾
Prices for the same job vary 30–50% between contractors. Never accept a verbal estimate — get everything in writing. The lowest bid is often NOT the best deal. A contractor who bids 30% below everyone else is likely cutting corners or planning change orders.
☐ Verify contractor license & insuranceLearn ▾
In Maryland, verify at mhic.maryland.gov. Ask for their license number and proof of general liability insurance. No license = no legal protection if something goes wrong. Don't skip this even if a friend refers them.
☐ Add 20% buffer to every estimateLearn ▾
Most renovations hit unexpected costs — hidden water damage, outdated wiring, supply delays. If contractor says $10K, budget $12K. This single rule prevents more renovation disasters than anything else.
☐ Never pay more than 30% upfrontLearn ▾
Legitimate contractors don't need full payment before starting. Structure payments in milestones: 30% start, 30% midpoint, 30% near completion, 10% final walkthrough approval. "Cash only" is a red flag.
☐ Estimate your full cost before calling anyoneLearn ▾
Know your number before you talk to a contractor. Use HomeFixCost to estimate by ZIP code so you know if their quote is fair — or if they're trying to overcharge you.

Frequently Asked Questions

What flooring is best for pets in 2026?▾
Luxury vinyl plank (LVP) is the top pick for pet owners — 100% waterproof, scratch-resistant, and much cheaper than hardwood. Look for a wear layer of at least 12 mil. Ask the tool above for a personalized recommendation based on your rooms.
Which renovation adds the most resale value in Maryland?▾
In Maryland's competitive market, minor kitchen remodels (110%+ ROI), garage door replacements (94% ROI), and curb appeal upgrades return the most per dollar. Big gut renovations rarely return their full cost. Use HomeFixCost to estimate your project before committing.
Is HomeStyle AI real AI?▾
It's a smart advice tool that matches your question to expert, pre-built home guidance — fast and free. It gives reliable general direction, but it isn't a replacement for a contractor or designer reviewing your actual home.
What paint colors are replacing gray in 2026?▾
Warm tones are taking over from cool grays. Soft sage green, warm white (like Benjamin Moore White Dove), warm beige/greige, and terracotta accents are the big replacements. Ask the tool above for specific room-by-room recommendations.
